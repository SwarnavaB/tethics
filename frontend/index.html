Why TETHICS

A legitimacy layer, not a marketing page

Most launch surfaces make it easy to impersonate a real project. TETHICS is built to answer a stricter question: who is actually authorized to represent this project onchain, and where is that decision recorded?

Founder-first verification

Projects propose themselves and attach proof material directly from the UI. Nothing depends on a private support desk or opaque moderation queue.

Native chain governance

EVM projects use EVM governance. Solana projects use Solana-native governance. The product surface is unified, but the authority model stays chain-correct.

Public review trail

Approvals, rejections, delegated reviewers, charity routing options, and review artifacts are intended to be inspectable rather than hidden behind a backend.

Built For Different Users

One protocol, three operating audiences

TETHICS is not just a verifier. It is a working system for founders, reviewers, and the public, each with distinct surfaces and responsibilities.

For founders

Propose projects, connect wallets, publish proof bundles, and maintain official asset records without relying on centralized launch venues to represent you correctly.

For curators and reviewers

Review pending proposals, delegate scoped roles, manage charity routing options, and publish legitimacy decisions through explicit governance controls.

For users and communities

Verify contracts, mints, creator wallets, and review artifacts from a public frontend designed to surface decisions rather than hide them.

How It Works

Protocol structure

TETHICS separates public coordination, project-level asset authorization, governance delegation, and frontend verification into explicit layers so the system stays inspectable as it grows.

Layer 1
Project Proposal
Founders connect wallets, submit project claims, upload public proof bundles, and anchor legitimacy requests through chain-native flows.
Layer 2
Review + Approval
Curators and delegated reviewers approve, reject, or revoke claims and assets. Decisions are tied to explicit authority roles rather than hidden operators.
Layer 3
Asset Registry
Official EVM tokens, Solana mints, creator wallets, and unwanted assets are recorded per project so verification is project-centric, not chain-siloed.
Layer 4
Public Verification
Users, wallets, communities, and explorers can verify current legitimacy status from the TETHICS frontend without relying on a private backend.

Trust Layer

Security and operating posture

The goal is not just legitimacy claims, but legitimacy claims under a governance and evidence model that can survive scrutiny.

Onchain role visibility

Reviewers, approvers, charity managers, and root authorities are meant to be explicit rather than implied by offchain moderation.

Public artifacts

Evidence bundles, review decisions, and authorization records are intended to resolve through durable public references rather than a hidden backend.

Chain-native execution

EVM and Solana use their own governance surfaces so the protocol does not fake cross-chain authority through a single backend coordinator.

Founder Flow

What a project owner actually does

The product entry is operational, not decorative. Founders are expected to move from proposal to review to asset approval inside the platform.

Propose the project

Connect the relevant wallet, submit the project slug and proof package, and anchor the claim through the appropriate EVM or Solana flow.

Undergo review

The initial curator or delegated reviewer validates the evidence bundle, linked wallets, and launch context before publishing any approval.

Authorize official assets

After approval, official contracts, mints, creator wallets, and related records become queryable inside the platform’s verification surfaces.

Respond to impersonation

Unwanted tokens or mints can be marked publicly, with review evidence and governance attribution visible to users and communities.

FAQ

Common questions before entering the platform

Is this a backend-heavy moderation product?

No. The intended model is static frontend plus onchain coordination plus public evidence artifacts. The legitimacy surface should be inspectable, not hidden in an admin backend.

Does TETHICS only support EVM projects?

No. EVM projects use EVM-native governance and Solana projects use Solana-native governance. The user-facing product is unified, but the authority flows stay chain-correct.

Who controls approvals at the beginning?

The initial curated phase is rooted in tethics.eth and tethics.sol, with explicit delegation paths for reviewers and other scoped roles over time.

Why redirect at all?

It does not. This page is the public-facing introduction to the protocol. Entering the application is an explicit user action through the CTA buttons.
